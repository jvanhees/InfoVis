Amsterdam, London, and Los Angeles are facing the impact of emerging trends among new technological innovations and increased connectivity.

The following slide illustrates the growth of companies that started in the ICT sector.

Events

1991: World Wide Web

CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research) introduces the World Wide Web to the public.

1992 - The first audio and video are distributed over the Internet. The phrase “surfing the Internet” is popularized.

1994 - Microsoft creates a Web browser for Windows 95.

1995 - SSL encryption was developed by Netscape, making it safer to conduct financial transactions online, which enables Internet shopping.

1997 - August 1, Steve Jobs returns to Apple Computer, Inc at Macworld in Boston. One week later, Microsoft buys a $150 million share of financially troubled Apple Computer.

1998: Google Search

The Google search engine is born. Now the most-used search engine on the Web.

1998 - The United States Department of Justice and 20 U.S. states file an antitrust case against Microsoft. America Online announces it will acquire Netscape Communications in a stock-for-stock transaction worth US $4.2 billion.

1999 - Microsoft releases Windows 98, Second Edition.

2000 - Founder Nina Brink takes under a lot of media attention Internet service World Online to the stock market. The expectations are high and many traders think they can get quickly rich with the shares.

2001: Dot-com bubble

The infamous Dot-com bubble that bursted in 2001. A massive rise of web-related stocks and companies.

2001 - 9/11 terrorist attacks in New York City.

2004: Web 2.0

Web 2.0 arrives and Facebook goes online and the era of social networking begins.

2005 - YouTube.com launches, bringing free online video hosting and sharing to the masses.

2006 - Twitter was created and launched. The service rapidly gained worldwide popularity.

2007: Financial crisis

The financial crisis forced a lot of companies to close. It is very clear that the crisis hits Amsterdam two years later.

2007 - The first iPhone was unveiled, which was almost wholly responsible for renewed interest in mobile web applications and design.

2010 - Facebook reaches 400 million active users.
